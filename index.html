About Me

My name is Erika, and I run experiments with a sample size of one. I am devoting my life right now to self-experimentation: placing myself in states of novel uncertainty that demand rapid adaptation and growth. I strive daily to harness my potential in every realm and make the most of my existence.

This past March, I had the crazy idea to host a co-living experience for women in tech with a girl I met on Twitter in a city I'd never been to. Three months later, there I was: living in San Francisco with 14 perfect strangers, rapidly immersed into an ecosystem of shockingly accomplished young people.

My second week in the city, I wrote a reflection detailing my impressions of the environment. I was pretty starstruck - amazed by the power of networks and the tangibility of things I could hardly fathom were within my reach.

Fast forward to July. I remember the night I cancelled my plane tickets home, the day I called my academic advisor and asked to take a year off. I danced in the freefall, wide-eyed and eager to take my newly ambiguous future head-on. I was not the same girl who came to San Francisco two months prior - I finally felt liberated, respected, and empowered to make the world my oyster.

My life has since taken an uncountable number of twists and turns. I cannot tell you how many iterations of myself I've met in the last five months. Its shocking to say, but me today would be unrecognizable to me even a month ago. I somehow know so much more and so much less about myself in this instant than I ever have.

Thanks for taking the time to learn a bit more about me and what I've done. There's more to my story, but believe me when I say:
I can't even begin to imagine how it'll be written.
